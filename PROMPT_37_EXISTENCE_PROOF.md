## Key Observations

### 1. Admissibility Structure
For p ‚â° 1 (mod 4), we have x_k = (p + m_k)/4.
- If m_k ‚â° 3 (mod 4), then p + m_k ‚â° 0 (mod 4), so x_k is an integer.
- Admissibility (gcd(x_k, m_k) = 1) fails iff some prime q | m_k also divides x_k.

### 2. The 23 Moduli Cover Different Prime Signatures
The moduli m_k have different prime factorizations:
- Primes: 3, 7, 11, 19, 23, 31, 47, 59, 67, 71, 79, 103, 107, 127, 167
- Composite: 15=3√ó5, 27=3¬≥, 39=3√ó13, 55=5√ó11, 87=3√ó29, 95=5√ó19, 119=7√ó17, 159=3√ó53

### 3. Quadratic Residue Structure
The 6 resistant classes mod 840 are {1, 121, 169, 289, 361, 529} - all perfect squares.
This is the Elsholtz-Tao obstruction: congruence constructions can't eliminate quadratic residues.

But K_COMPLETE **does** cover these classes empirically, just with unbounded d.

### 4. Factorization Diversity
x_k varies with k, so different k values produce different factorizations of x_k.
If x_k has prime factors {q‚ÇÅ, ..., q_r}, then H_{x_k} = ‚ü®q‚ÇÅ, ..., q_r‚ü© mod m_k.

The conjecture is that across the 23 choices of k, at least one produces an H_{x_k} containing -x_k.

## Possible Proof Approaches

### Approach 1: Density/Measure Argument
Show that the "failure set" (primes where no k works) has density 0, then prove it's actually empty.

### Approach 2: Chinese Remainder Theorem Coverage
The 23 moduli m_k are coprime in various combinations. Their lcm is large (~10^17).
Perhaps every residue class mod this lcm has at least one admissible k with a valid witness.

### Approach 3: Pigeonhole on Group Structure
For each prime p, consider the 23 groups H_{x_k} ‚äÜ (‚Ñ§/m_k ‚Ñ§)*.
Show that the "targets" -x_k cannot all miss their respective groups.

### Approach 4: Character Sum Bounds
Use character sums to bound the probability that -x_k ‚àâ H_{x_k} for all k simultaneously.

## What Would Constitute a Proof?

A complete proof would show:

**Theorem:** For every prime p ‚â° 1 (mod 4), there exists k ‚àà K_COMPLETE such that -x_k ‚àà H_{x_k}, where H_{x_k} is the subgroup of (‚Ñ§/m_k ‚Ñ§)* generated by the prime factors of x_k.
